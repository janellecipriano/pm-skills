# Stakeholder Communication Plan

Identify everyone who matters to a program and define a deliberate plan for keeping each informed and bought-in.

## When to use
- Starting a program/project and setting up communications.
- Diagnosing why stakeholders feel out of the loop or misaligned.

## Inputs to gather
- The program and its phase.
- Known stakeholders/groups (sponsors, leadership, partner teams, users, vendors).
- Existing meetings/channels and any sensitivities.

## Process
1. **Identify stakeholders** — individuals and groups affected by or influential to the work.
2. **Assess each** on **interest** (how much they care) and **influence** (how much power), → quadrant:
   - High influence / high interest → **manage closely** (frequent, tailored).
   - High influence / low interest → **keep satisfied** (concise, periodic).
   - Low influence / high interest → **keep informed** (regular updates).
   - Low influence / low interest → **monitor** (minimal).
3. For each, define: **what** they need, **why** (their concern), **cadence**, **channel**, **owner**, and **format**.
4. Map to concrete **vehicles** (steering review, weekly status, newsletter, demo, 1:1) — reuse `status-report`/`exec-summary` as the content.
5. Note **risks** (skeptics, blockers) and an engagement approach for each.

## Output format
```
## Stakeholder Map — {{program}}
| Stakeholder | Influence | Interest | Strategy | Key concern |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Communication Plan
| Audience | What they get | Cadence | Channel | Format | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```
Keep it actionable — every row should map to a real, scheduled touchpoint.
